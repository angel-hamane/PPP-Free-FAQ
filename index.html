Legal / Education Clarification

What’s the difference between probate and probate litigation?

General probate handles administration of estates (distributing assets, paying debts). Probate litigation involves legal disputes—such as contesting a trust or will—brought before the court for resolution.

What happens if I miss the statute of limitations deadline?

Generally, the court will not allow you to file after the deadline. This is why learning and acting within the statute of limitations is critical. For specifics, consult an attorney.

Why is “standing” important?

Standing determines who has the legal right to bring a case. If you don’t meet the requirements, the court will dismiss your petition.
